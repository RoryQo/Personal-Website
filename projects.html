PROJECTS

Tuition Variation Model, Undergraduate Research Capstone

  • Developed a predictive model in R to analyze tuition rates for public and private four-year universities in the U.S., utilizing institutional, quality of life, and crime metrics to explain tuition variability.
  • Achieved over 85% of variability in tuition rates accounted for, with national ranking identified as the most significant predictor, demonstrating that prestigious institutions leverage their rankings to charge higher tuition.
  • Collected data from three public datasets encompassing 60 observations from top U.S. universities, including metrics such as institutional control, national rank, and median income.
  • Constructed a comprehensive linear regression model, employing leave-one-out cross-validation (LOOCV) to validate predictive capabilities and ensuring robustness against overfitting.
  • Conducted thorough data wrangling to clean and prepare datasets, addressing missing values and ensuring appropriate variable encoding.
  • Applied the model to the University of Pittsburgh, revealing that out-of-state students pay approximately $9,000 above expected tuition, highlighting the model's practical applicability for prospective students assessing educational investments.

CPI Mini Research Capstone

  • Analyzed the Consumer Price Index (CPI) specifically for college students by evaluating essential products and services they typically consume, resulting in a custom CPI calculation based on selected items and their respective weights across different U.S. regions
  • Collected price data through API retrieval for three regions (Northeast, Midwest, South) covering a five-year period (January 2017 to January 2022) to support the CPI calculations
  • Implemented functions to calculate inflation rates based on the custom CPI, facilitating insights into the sensitivity of student-related expenses to inflation changes
  • Conducted a comprehensive analysis highlighting that while the custom CPI is less robust than the general CPI, it captures inflation trends that significantly affect college students, particularly for essential items like gas, recreation, and rent
  • Developed a comparative graph visualizing inflation rates of the custom CPI against the general CPI over various lag periods, emphasizing the differing experiences of college students compared to the broader population

Age Correlation in Married Couples, Bayesian Analysis Project

  • Analyzed the relationship between the ages of husbands and wives using data from 100 sampled U.S. married couples, aiming to hypothesize a semiconjugate prior.
  • Generated predictive datasets through random sampling from established distributions, confirming a strong positive correlation between the ages.
  • Employed Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) methods to estimate mean ages and the correlation coefficient, creating new confidence intervals for both.
  • Demonstrated that the Bayesian approach provided narrower confidence intervals for average ages, reducing them by over a year compared to frequentist methods.
  • Highlighted the advantages of Bayesian methods in estimating correlations, achieving a 2% reduction in the correlation coefficient's confidence interval.
  • Utilized R for data analysis, focusing on visualizing relationships and employing statistical techniques to enhance the reliability of findings on age dynamics in marriages.

Country Aid Mini Project

  • Analyzed socio-economic and health indicators to categorize countries most in need of humanitarian assistance for NGOs.
  • Conducted a correlation analysis and utilized Principal Component Analysis (PCA) to identify key factors affecting country development, including child mortality, income, life expectancy, and health expenditure.
  • Determined three optimal clusters through clustering techniques, finding that Partitioning Around Medoids (PAM) clustering outperformed K-means due to its resilience to outliers.
  • Identified Cluster 1, comprising 51 countries with the most critical needs, highlighting urgent areas for intervention, such as high child mortality and low average income.
  • Created visualizations to compare key factors across clusters, effectively illustrating disparities and underscoring the need for targeted interventions in Cluster 1.
  • Developed an R workflow for data analysis, including loading libraries, performing exploratory data analysis, and executing clustering methods to assess humanitarian needs.

Students' Adaptability to Online Learning, Data Science Project

  • Investigated students' adaptability to online learning by applying data wrangling, visualization, and machine learning techniques to understand key influencing factors and predict adaptability levels.
  • Achieved approximately 93% accuracy in predicting adaptability using advanced machine learning models, including non-linear Support Vector Machine (SVM) and Random Forest, outperforming basic models like Naive Bayes.
  • Identified significant factors influencing adaptability, such as age, class duration, gender, and education level, while finding device type and load-shedding to be less influential.
  • Created insightful visualizations using Python libraries to analyze distributions and relationships among demographic factors, enhancing understanding of student adaptability.
  • Prepared data by encoding categorical variables, scaling features, and conducting hyperparameter tuning to optimize model performance for accuracy, precision, and recall.
