Tuition Variation Model, Undergraduate Research Capstone

Project Summary: Developed a predictive model in R to analyze tuition rates for public and private universities in the U.S., utilizing various metrics to explain tuition variability.

Technologies Used: R, Linear Regression, dplyr, caret, ggplot2

Detailed Description:

  • Problem Statement: Understanding the factors that influence tuition rates across different universities.
  • Solution: Created a linear regression model to predict tuition variability.
  • Process:
    • Collected data from three public datasets, focusing on 60 universities across the U.S.
    • Conducted data wrangling to clean and prepare datasets, including addressing missing values and ensuring appropriate variable encoding.
    • Explored institutional, quality of life, and crime metrics to understand their relationship with tuition rates.
    • Constructed a comprehensive linear regression model
    • performed stepwise model selection to identify the most significant predictors of tuition variability.
    • Validated its predictive capabilities using leave-one-out cross-validation (LOOCV).
    • Applied the model to analyze the University of Pittsburgh

Results and Impact: The model accounts for 85% of the variability in tuition rates, with university rank being a key factor driving these differences. This indicates that more prestigious institutions can command higher tuition fees. Notably, other commonly cited metrics, such as student-to-faculty ratios and graduation rates, did not significantly influence tuition predictions, suggesting that students are increasingly paying for prestige rather than institutional investment. The model's effectiveness in predicting tuition rates demonstrates its practical applicability, offering valuable insights for prospective students assessing their educational investments.

Challenges Faced: Managed data quality issues during collection and cleaning.

Future Improvements: Explore the inclusion of additional factors like financial aid offerings.

Age Correlation in Married Couples, Bayesian Analysis Challenge

Project Summary: Utilized Bayesian methods to analyze the relationship between the ages of husbands and wives, from 100
sampled U.S. married couples. This approach enhanced the usability of the analysis by providing more reliable estimates and narrower
confidence intervals, surpassing the limitations of traditional methods.

Technologies Used: R, Bayesian Analysis, Stochastic Processes (MCMC), rstan (for Bayesian modeling)

Detailed Description:

  • Problem Statement: Due to limited data, traditional methods yielded wide confidence intervals, making it challenging
    to accurately understand age dynamics in marriages.
  • Solution: Utilized Bayesian methods to analyze age correlation.
  • Process:
    • Hypothesized an Inverse Wishart distribution for variance and a Multivariate Normal distribution for the mean.
    • Generated predictive datasets through random sampling from hypothesized distributions, confirming a strong positive
      correlation between ages.
    • Confirmed our hypothesis by comparing predictive datasets through scatter plots to the original data, demonstrating that
      they exhibited the same relationship.
    • Employed Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) methods to estimate mean ages and the correlation coefficient.
    • Created confidence intervals for both estimates, demonstrating the advantages of Bayesian methods over traditional approaches.

Results and Impact: The limited sample size and lack of prior information about the variables resulted in relatively wide confidence intervals using
standard frequentist approaches. In contrast, the Bayesian method reduced the confidence interval for
the average ages of husbands and wives by over a year, while the confidence interval for the
correlation coefficient was narrowed by 2% compared to the frequentist method at the same alpha level. These findings
underscore the advantages of Bayesian methods, particularly in providing narrower confidence intervals and more
accurate estimations of correlations. By effectively leveraging prior information,
the Bayesian approach significantly enhances the reliability of our analysis regarding age dynamics in marriages.

Challenges Faced: Required a solid understanding of Bayesian statistics for accurate implementation.

Future Improvements: Explore larger datasets for broader analysis.

Effect of Seasonal Migration in Bangladesh, Research Reproduction

Project Summary: Replicated Bryan et al. (2014) analysis on seasonal migration's impact on household consumption in rural Bangladesh.

Technologies Used: R, Regression Analysis, Instrumental Variables, Fixed Effects, dplyr, RCT

Detailed Description:

  • Problem Statement: Investigate how seasonal migration subsidies impact household consumption during the lean season.
  • Solution: Replicated the randomized control trial (RCT) analysis using R to estimate treatment effects on migration and consumption, and apply an instrumental variable approach to isolate causal effects.
  • Process:
    • Loaded and merged data from two rounds of household surveys (pre- and post-treatment)
    • Conducted regression analysis to estimate the intention-to-treat (ITT) effects on baseline outcomes like savings and caloric intake
    • Performed first-stage regression to examine the impact of the treatment on migration
    • Estimated the effect of treatment on household consumption using a reduced form regression
    • Used an instrumental variable (IV) approach to estimate the Local Average Treatment Effect (LATE) of migration on consumption

Results and Impact: The LATE estimates revealed that migration induced by the treatment increased household consumption by approximately 374 takka, offering insights into the potential for migration subsidies to improve welfare in rural areas.

Challenges Faced: Addressed issues of endogeneity by using treatment as an instrument for migration.

Literature Review on the Economic Impact of Terrorism

Project Summary: This review analyzes the economic effects of terrorism, focusing on its influence on business performance and GDP growth across various sectors and regions.

Key Findings:

  • Economic Declines: Terrorism leads to significant decreases in revenues and GDP. For instance, Bayter (2021) reported a 2% to 4% annual revenue drop in France for three years post-attacks, while Abadie and Gardeazabal (2003) noted a 10% decline in GDP per capita in the Basque Country.
  • Perceived Risk and Relocation: Fear from terrorism can drive businesses to relocate, as seen after 9/11 in Chicago, where vacancies increased in prime office spaces.
  • Sector-Specific Impacts: The agricultural and manufacturing sectors are particularly vulnerable. Ali (2010) highlighted the decline in Pakistan's agriculture and the manufacturing sector's lowest growth in a decade due to terrorism-related disruptions.
  • Heterogeneous Effects: Transnational terrorism has a greater negative impact on economic growth in developing countries, emphasizing the complex nature of terrorism's economic effects.

Results and Impact: Terrorism disrupts business performance and economic growth, necessitating targeted policies to mitigate its impacts and protect economies. Understanding these relationships is vital for strategic planning and effective responses.
