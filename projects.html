PROJECTS

Tuition Variation Model, Undergraduate Research Capstone

  • Developed a predictive model in R to analyze tuition rates for public and private four-year universities in the U.S., utilizing institutional, quality of life, and crime metrics to explain tuition variability.
  • Achieved over 85% of variability in tuition rates accounted for, with national ranking identified as the most significant predictor.
  • Collected data from three public datasets encompassing 60 observations from top U.S. universities.
  • Constructed a comprehensive linear regression model, employing leave-one-out cross-validation (LOOCV) to validate predictive capabilities.
  • Conducted thorough data wrangling to clean and prepare datasets.
  • Applied the model to the University of Pittsburgh, revealing that out-of-state students pay approximately $9,000 above expected tuition.

CPI Mini Research Capstone

  • Analyzed the Consumer Price Index (CPI) specifically for college students, resulting in a custom CPI calculation.
  • Collected price data through API retrieval for three regions covering a five-year period.
  • Implemented functions to calculate inflation rates based on the custom CPI.
  • Conducted a comprehensive analysis highlighting that while the custom CPI is less robust than the general CPI, it captures inflation trends affecting college students.
  • Developed a comparative graph visualizing inflation rates of the custom CPI against the general CPI.

Age Correlation in Married Couples, Bayesian Analysis Project

  • Analyzed the relationship between the ages of husbands and wives using data from 100 sampled U.S. married couples.
  • Generated predictive datasets through random sampling, confirming a strong positive correlation.
  • Employed Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) methods to estimate mean ages and the correlation coefficient.
  • Demonstrated that the Bayesian approach provided narrower confidence intervals for average ages.
  • Utilized R for data analysis, focusing on visualizing relationships and employing statistical techniques.
