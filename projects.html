Tuition Variation Model, Undergraduate Research Capstone

Project Summary: Developed a predictive model in R to analyze tuition rates for public and private universities in the U.S., utilizing various metrics to explain tuition variability.

Technologies Used: R, Linear Regression, Data Visualization

Detailed Description:

  • Problem Statement: Understanding the factors that influence tuition rates across different universities.
  • Solution: Created a linear regression model to predict tuition variability.
  • Process:
    • Collected data from three public datasets, focusing on 60 universities across the U.S.
    • Conducted data wrangling to clean and prepare datasets, including addressing missing values and ensuring appropriate variable encoding.
    • Explored institutional, quality of life, and crime metrics to understand their relationship with tuition rates.
    • Constructed a comprehensive linear regression model and validated its predictive capabilities using leave-one-out cross-validation (LOOCV).
    • Applied the model to analyze the University of Pittsburgh, highlighting the financial impact on out-of-state students.

Results and Impact: Achieved over 85% of variability in tuition rates accounted for, identifying national ranking as a significant predictor.

Challenges Faced: Managed data quality issues during collection and cleaning.

Future Improvements: Explore the inclusion of additional factors like financial aid offerings.

Navigating Inflation: A Custom CPI Analysis for College Students, Course Research Capstone

Project Summary: Analyzed the Consumer Price Index (CPI) for college students, creating a custom CPI calculation based on essential products and services.

Technologies Used: Python, pandas, numpy

Detailed Description:

  • Problem Statement: Traditional CPI does not accurately reflect college students' consumption patterns.
  • Solution: Developed a custom CPI to better reflect the costs that impact students.
  • Process:
    • Collected price data through API retrieval for essential products and services in three U.S. regions over a five-year period (January 2017 to January 2022).
    • Calculated weighted averages for each product category based on student consumption patterns.
    • Implemented functions to compute inflation rates using the custom CPI, enabling analysis of price changes over time.
    • Conducted a comparative analysis to highlight significant trends in inflation affecting college students.
    • Developed visualizations to illustrate inflation rates of the custom CPI against the general CPI across different lag periods.

Results and Impact: Highlighted significant inflation trends affecting college students, particularly in essential categories.

Challenges Faced: Encountered limitations with data availability for specific items.

Future Improvements: Consider expanding the analysis to include more product categories and regions.

Age Correlation in Married Couples, Bayesian Analysis Project

Project Summary: Analyzed the relationship between the ages of husbands and wives using data from 100 sampled U.S. married couples.

Technologies Used: R, Bayesian Analysis, MCMC

Detailed Description:

  • Problem Statement: Understanding age dynamics in marriages.
  • Solution: Utilized Bayesian methods to analyze age correlation.
  • Process:
    • Gathered data from a sample of 100 married couples, recording their ages.
    • Explored various distributions to determine the best fit for age data.
    • Generated predictive datasets through random sampling from established distributions, confirming a strong positive correlation between ages.
    • Employed Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) methods to estimate mean ages and the correlation coefficient.
    • Created confidence intervals for both estimates, demonstrating the advantages of Bayesian methods over traditional approaches.

Results and Impact: Demonstrated narrower confidence intervals using Bayesian methods, showcasing their effectiveness.

Challenges Faced: Required a solid understanding of Bayesian statistics for accurate implementation.

Future Improvements: Explore larger datasets for broader analysis.

Country Needs Assessment for Humanitarian Aid, Data Science Mini Project

Project Summary: Analyzed socio-economic and health indicators to identify countries most in need of humanitarian assistance.

Technologies Used: R, PCA, Clustering

Detailed Description:

  • Problem Statement: Assessing humanitarian needs based on various indicators.
  • Solution: Used PCA and clustering to categorize countries by urgency of aid.
  • Process:
    • Collected data on socio-economic and health indicators from reliable sources.
    • Conducted correlation analysis to identify key factors affecting country development, such as child mortality and income levels.
    • Applied Principal Component Analysis (PCA) to reduce dimensionality and extract significant features from the dataset.
    • Determined three optimal clusters using clustering techniques, comparing the effectiveness of Partitioning Around Medoids (PAM) with K-means.
    • Created visualizations to compare key factors across clusters, effectively illustrating disparities.

Results and Impact: Identified 51 countries in Cluster 1 with urgent needs for intervention.

Challenges Faced: Data availability and reliability were critical factors.

Future Improvements: Incorporate real-time data for more dynamic assessments.
